The French Revolution

1789 - 1799

A decade that changed the course of history, overthrowing centuries of monarchy and establishing the foundations of modern democratic society.

Revolutionary Timeline

Estates-General Convenes

May 5, 1789

First meeting since 1614, representing the three estates of French society

Tennis Court Oath

June 20, 1789

Third Estate pledges to draft a new constitution

Storming of the Bastille

July 14, 1789

Symbolic beginning of the French Revolution

Declaration of Rights

August 26, 1789

Declaration of the Rights of Man and of the Citizen adopted

First French Republic

September 22, 1792

Monarchy abolished, Republic established

Reign of Terror

1793-1794

Period of violent political purges and mass executions

Napoleon's Coup

November 9, 1799

Napoleon Bonaparte seizes power, ending the Directory

Key Figures

Louis XVI

King of France

Last King of France before the revolution, executed in 1793

Marie Antoinette

Queen of France

Austrian-born Queen, symbol of royal excess, executed in 1793

Maximilien Robespierre

Revolutionary Leader

Leader of the Jacobins and architect of the Reign of Terror

Jacques Necker

Finance Minister

Swiss banker who served as Director-General of Finance

Georges Danton

Revolutionary Leader

Moderate revolutionary leader, eventually executed during Reign of Terror

Napoleon Bonaparte

Military Leader

Rose to power after the revolution, became Emperor of France

Causes of Revolution

Economic Causes

  • Financial crisis due to involvement in American Revolutionary War
  • Heavy taxation burden on Third Estate
  • Food shortages and high bread prices
  • Inefficient tax system favoring nobility and clergy

Social Causes

  • Inequality between the three estates
  • Rise of bourgeoisie demanding political representation
  • Enlightenment ideas spreading among educated classes
  • Resentment against privileged classes

Political Causes

  • Absolute monarchy lacking popular support
  • Weak leadership of Louis XVI
  • Lack of representation for Third Estate
  • Influence of Enlightenment political philosophy

Major Events

Storming of the Bastille

July 14, 1789

Marked the beginning of the French Revolution and became a symbol of popular uprising against tyranny

Great Fear

July-August 1789

Rural uprising that led to the abolition of feudalism in France

Women's March on Versailles

October 5-6, 1789

Forced the royal family to move from Versailles to Paris, bringing them under closer scrutiny

Execution of Louis XVI

January 21, 1793

Marked the definitive end of the monarchy and intensified conflict with European powers

Impact and Consequences

Political Impact

  • End of absolute monarchy in France
  • Establishment of democratic ideals
  • Rise of nationalism across Europe
  • Influence on democratic movements worldwide

Social Impact

  • Abolition of feudalism and serfdom
  • Declaration of human rights
  • Social mobility based on merit rather than birth
  • Secularization of society

Economic Impact

  • Abolition of guilds and trade restrictions
  • Land redistribution from nobility to peasants
  • Introduction of metric system
  • Economic modernization and industrialization
